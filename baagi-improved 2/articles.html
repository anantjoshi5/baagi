Articles & Analysis

In‑depth explorations of governance, law and technology

Permit Raj: How Bureaucracy Feeds Corruption

India’s labyrinth of licences and approvals has spawned a culture of bribery and delay. We examine how ambiguous rules and discretionary powers create opportunities for rent‑seeking and show how codified, digital processes can end the “speed money” economy.

Police Stops and Discretion: Lessons from Europe

Law‑enforcement officers often rely on instinct when stopping and searching individuals. Drawing on European survey data, this article highlights disparities in how minority communities experience police stops and proposes a framework for objective, rule‑driven policing.

Smart Contracts for Land Titles: Securing Property Rights

Blockchain and smart contracts promise faster, more transparent land registries. We explore a pilot project in Europe, detailing how tamper‑proof ledgers can protect property rights, reduce fraud and save millions in administrative costs.

Trade Agreements and Prosperity

Trade agreements open markets and lower prices, enhancing freedom and prosperity. This article draws on Cato scholarship to show how free trade lifts living standards and reinforces the rule of law in global commerce.

Free Markets and the Great Enrichment

Economic freedom has lifted billions out of poverty, creating an explosion of prosperity dubbed the Great Enrichment. We explain why defending free markets remains vital and highlight voices amplifying the case for capitalism.

Restoring Financial Privacy

Financial privacy has eroded under decades of hidden surveillance. This article explains how existing laws undermine privacy, surveys public concern and proposes reforms to restore constitutional protections.

CBDC and the Threat to Financial Privacy

A central bank digital currency would give government unprecedented access to our financial lives. Drawing on Cato’s analysis, we explore why a CBDC could entrench surveillance and erode liberty.

School Choice: Empowering Families

From vouchers to education savings accounts, school choice programs let education funding follow students. We trace the rise of school choice, describe different models and examine its impact on families across America.

The Abundance Agenda: Red Tape and Tax Reform

The abundance agenda urges policymakers to cut red tape so that more homes, energy and innovation can flourish. But without fiscal reform, regulatory changes will fall short. This article explains why tax policy must accompany deregulation.

Property Rights and Economic Growth

Secure property rights and clear land records are engines of prosperity. We review research linking cadastres and defined property rights to higher GDP and explain why libertarians champion strong property protections.

The Costs of the War on Drugs

America’s drug war has inflicted enormous human and economic costs. Drawing on Cato’s policy analysis, we detail the toll on lives, liberties and public finances and outline a path toward reform.

Holding the Fed Accountable

The Federal Reserve was created to stabilize the economy but has presided over crises and inflation. We argue for rules‑based monetary policy and greater accountability to restrain the Fed’s discretion.

Immigration and Economic Growth

Immigrants are workers, entrepreneurs and taxpayers who drive growth and stave off population decline. This article presents evidence that a freer, more orderly immigration system would benefit Americans and the economy.
