In‑depth explorations of governance, law and technology

Permit Raj: How Bureaucracy Feeds Corruption

India’s labyrinth of licences and approvals has spawned a culture of bribery and delay. We examine how ambiguous rules and discretionary powers create opportunities for rent‑seeking and show how codified, digital processes can end the “speed money” economy.

Police Stops and Discretion: Lessons from Europe

Law‑enforcement officers often rely on instinct when stopping and searching individuals. Drawing on European survey data, this article highlights disparities in how minority communities experience police stops and proposes a framework for objective, rule‑driven policing.

Smart Contracts for Land Titles: Securing Property Rights

Blockchain and smart contracts promise faster, more transparent land registries. We explore a pilot project in Europe, detailing how tamper‑proof ledgers can protect property rights, reduce fraud and save millions in administrative costs.
