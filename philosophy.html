Limited Government and Free Markets

A libertarian perspective recognises that power tends to corrupt. When governments expand beyond their core functions, they create opportunities for rentâ€‘seeking, discretion and abuse. We advocate for limited government focused on defining and enforcing clear rules. This includes protecting contracts and property rights, maintaining public order and providing a neutral framework within which individuals and businesses can freely cooperate.

Free markets enable voluntary exchange and innovation. They harness dispersed knowledge and allow individuals to pursue their own goals without coercing others. By contrast, discretionary regulation can stifle enterprise and favour insiders. Baagi supports policies that minimise arbitrary interventions and instead rely on predictable rules applied transparently through digital means.
